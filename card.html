Gray wolf

Wolves are excellent hunters and have been found to be living in more places in the world than any other mammal except humans.

  • Scientific name: Canis lupus
  • Average length: 105–160 cm
  • Running gait: 50–60 km/h
  • Average lifespan: 5–6 years

The gray wolf (Canis lupus), also known as the timber wolf or western wolf, is a canine native to the wilderness and remote areas of Eurasia and North America. It is the largest extant member of its family, with males averaging 43–45 kg (95–99 lb) and females 36–38.5 kg (79–85 lb).
